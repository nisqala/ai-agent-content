## Run 1

### Hook text

(That room is empty. But it's being knocked on.) — 6 words, present tense.

Alternates if you want to A/B:

- Semua sudah tidur. Ketukan terus. (Everyone's asleep. The knocking won't stop.)
- Ketukannya datang dari dalam. (The knocking is coming from inside.)

Set high-contrast off-white, bottom-third, no outline, hold ~1.2s. Let it sit on a near-static frame so the read lands before any motion.

### Visual brief (3 detik pertama)
Locked-off medium shot down a narrow kampung-house hallway: bare teraso floor, a closed teak-veneer door at the end, a single kerosene lamp on a low side-table throwing the only light from frame-left — green-amber pooling on the floorboards, the far half of the corridor dropping into crushed, readable black. A wall clock ticks at the edge of the lamp's reach, second hand visible. Frame is dead still for the first two seconds — a child's plastic sandal lies on its side near the door, condensation fogging the lower glass of a louvre window. On the third second, a single soft knock from behind the closed door makes the lamp flame lean once, and nothing else moves. 16mm grain over everything, subtle vignette pulling the corners darker, deep focus so the dark end of the hall stays a threat and not a smudge.

### Mood/atmosphere brief
The whole piece lives in a cold, desaturated kampung house at the dead middle of the night — one Fincher LUT, no grade drift, warmth allowed only in frame via the kerosene lamp and a dying tungsten bulb in the back room. Light is the escalation channel: the lamp burns lower across beats, the bulb does its on–off–on, and pre-dawn blue starts bleeding through the louvres only in the final aftermath. Sound is the spine — long stretches of true silence broken by the knock, which must stay consistent and patient (same three-tap rhythm, same muffled timbre, never a sudden bang) so the dread is in its refusal to stop rather than its volume; layer in the clock, a kipas angin's slow oscillation, distant ronda whistle, the house settling. Pacing is restraint-then-collapse: the camera stays locked and the protagonist quietly rules out every living explanation — checks the kids' room, the back kitchen, calls a name and gets no answer — until the source of the knocking is the one room they were certain was empty. No flailing, no reveal the build didn't earn; the payoff is recognition, not a face. Grain, vignette, and 1/50 motion-feel throughout so nothing reads sterile.

### Hook text
KETUKANNYA DATANG DARI DALAM

### Visual brief (3 detik pertama)
Static wide shot of a modest Southeast Asian living room at 12:07 AM. A muted television glows blue in the corner. A standing fan oscillates slowly. The front door is visible at the far end of the frame. No people are present. On a small wooden table sits a half-finished cup of tea. For two full seconds, absolutely nothing happens. Then: TOK... TOK... TOK... The sound is close and heavy. The cup vibrates slightly. The front door does not move. The knock clearly sounds as if it comes from somewhere deeper inside the house, beyond a dark hallway leading toward the bedrooms.

### Mood/atmosphere brief
The video should feel like an ordinary Malaysian/Indonesian home after everyone has gone to sleep—quiet enough that small sounds become important. The fear comes from geography: viewers instantly understand that a knock from outside is normal, but a knock from somewhere inside an apparently empty house is wrong. Each escalation should be subtle and physical rather than supernatural: the hallway light flickers on–off–on, a bedroom door that was open is later half-closed, the fan pauses for one rotation, another set of knocks comes from a different room. The camera never rushes toward the source and never reveals a ghost. Instead, it behaves like a nervous witness trapped in the house. By the final beat, the audience should realize the knocking is moving closer room by room, following a path through the house toward the camera's position. The last knock lands from directly behind the camera, implying something has reached the viewer's space without ever showing what it is.

### Hook text
KETUKAN ITU DARI DALAM

### Visual brief (3 detik pertama)
Static wide shot of a modest Indonesian living room at 12:07 AM. A single yellow tungsten bulb hangs above a dining table, leaving the corners of the room swallowed by darkness. In the foreground, an old standing fan slowly oscillates. Beyond it, a closed bedroom door sits at the end of a narrow hallway. Nothing moves except the fan and a curtain near a half-open window. After one full second of silence, three slow knocks echo from behind the bedroom door. The camera does not react. The room remains unchanged. The unsettling detail: the bedroom door is locked from the outside with a rusty padlock. low-key single-source practical lighting, desaturated cool palette with green-amber shadows, crushed blacks, negative space, locked precise framing, deep focus, 16mm film grain, subtle vignette, cinematic

### Mood/atmosphere brief
The fear comes from certainty collapsing. The audience immediately understands a simple fact: nobody should be inside that room. The video unfolds through observation rather than action. Each new knock arrives from a different position within the locked room—first near the door, then deeper inside, then seemingly from the ceiling. The bulb briefly dims and recovers, not dramatically, but enough to make viewers question whether they saw movement in the hallway. Ambient sound dominates: fan motor hum, distant motorcycles, a barking dog outside, wood settling in an old house. No music. No visible entity. The escalation is purely spatial and logical—the impossible geography of the knocks. The final beat reveals a framed family photograph on the wall. In the photo, the same bedroom door stands open. A figure appears inside the room, staring toward the camera. Another slow knock sounds immediately after the viewer notices it, implying the source has been there all along and is now aware of being seen.
